## 1. The starting point: a different architecture

Standard cosmology (ΛCDM) describes a universe born from a Big Bang, expanding, containing
dark matter and dark energy as separate ingredients added by hand. Every mystery has its
own parameter: inflation solves the horizon problem, baryogenesis explains the matter-antimatter
asymmetry, DM is a hypothetical particle, DE is a cosmological constant of unknown origin.

This model starts from a radically different hypothesis: **our universe is a membrane (brane)
living on the event horizon of a five-dimensional black hole**. Not a metaphor — literally.
All matter, all forces, the expansion itself, are the holographic imprint of what falls into
that black hole, written on its surface.

The black hole is immersed in a five-dimensional Anti-de Sitter (AdS) space — a space with
constant negative curvature, like an immense gravitational bowl. And the black hole rotates
on **two independent axes**, with spins a₁ = 0.222 L and a₂ = 0.135 L (L is the bulk scale,
fixed by the observed cosmological constant). This asymmetry — a₁ ≠ a₂ — is the engine
of everything.

## 2. The flux: the engine of the model

The black hole grows because it receives **energy flux from the bulk** — null dust, null
radiation falling toward the horizon from all extra-dimensional directions. This flux has
the shape of a bell curve in time: it rises, reaches a peak, then declines. Today we are
on the declining side.

**Parallel with ΛCDM:** in standard cosmology, expansion is driven by the energy content
of the universe (matter, radiation, DE). Here expansion is driven by the black hole
accretion flux. The Friedmann equations on the brane (derived from Israel junction
conditions) contain the accretion term as a source — not as a parameter, but as dynamics.

The flux is not derivable from first principles (the master node of the model): we do not
know the microscopic distribution of matter in the bulk before it falls. But this is not
a limitation — it is a consequence of **holographic scrambling**: the horizon destroys
microscopic details and returns only macroscopic information (mass, spin). The flux is
reconstructed a posteriori from DESI data, as reverse engineering: not "this is the shape
of the bulk", but "this is the shape the bulk must have for the brane to produce the
universe we observe".

And the model is **robust**: tests with flux discretised into N packets show that the
Friedmann equation integrates and damps discontinuities. With N ≥ 15 the error falls to 5%.
The model does not depend on the continuous nature of the flux.

## 3. Two spins, three channels: the entire structure of matter

From the two spins, three physically distinct quantities are constructed:

**S = a₁² + a₂²** — the isotropic part. Carries mass and energy. It is the monopole.

**D = a₁² − a₂²** — the anisotropic part. Carries the quadrupole, angular structure,
electroweak forces, chirality. Changes sign at the frame-dragging inversion.

**R = φ₁ − φ₂** — the relative phase. Carries correlations, colour (the Cartan structure
of SU(3)), the position relative to the black hole. Carries no energy — carries information.

Each type of matter "reads" some of these coordinates and not others:

| Type | Channels | Acquires |
|------|----------|----------|
| Dark matter | S only | Mass — no gauge |
| Photon | D only | EW fields — no mass |
| Leptons/EW | S + D | Mass + EW forces |
| Baryons | S + D + R | Mass + EW + colour |

**The most elegant feature:** with equal spins (a₁ = a₂), D = 0. No quadrupole, no
chirality, no electroweak forces, no colour. The entire structure of the Standard Model
descends from a single asymmetry: the two spins are different.

## 4. Chirality: the problem Kaluza-Klein could not solve

In classical extra-dimension theories (Kaluza-Klein, Randall-Sundrum), fermion localisation

In this model something different happens. The frame-dragging — the rotational dragging of

This is the most structurally original feature of the model. In RS publications, the
Pöschl-Teller is designed by the author. Here it is an output of the pure Myers-Perry metric.

And chirality is **universal**: the inversion is at r_h = 0.173 L, in the remote past of
the black hole. All matter forms afterwards, on the same side of the inversion, with the
same sign. There is no "choice" for each particle — the geometry has already chosen for all.

**Parallel with ΛCDM:** the Standard Model inserts chirality by hand as a property of gauge
representations (only SU(2)_L, not SU(2)_R). Here it emerges from geometry without
additional ingredients.

## 6. Dark matter: many and simple

The formation-cost mechanism:

Each type of matter has a "geometric cost" proportional to the product of the dimensions
of the channels it acquires:
- DM: S only → cost = 1
- Leptons: S+D → cost = 1×2 = 2
- Baryons: S+D+R → cost = 1×2×3 = 6

DM is the simplest — it forms in greater abundance than baryons. Before annihilation:
n_DM/n_bar = 6. After (baryons annihilate with antimatter, only the fraction asym = 0.178
survives):

**n_DM / n_bar_survived = 6 / 0.178 = 33.7**

From Ω_DM/Ω_bar = (m_DM/m_bar) × 33.7 = 5.41 (observed):

**m_DM = 150.5 MeV — approximately the QCD scale**

This number was not put in by hand. It emerges from the product of four quantities:
the observed value 5.41, the proton mass, the geometric asymmetry 0.178, and the
cost ratio 6. The QCD scale (Λ_QCD ~ 150-200 MeV) comes out without having been inserted.

DM is **invisible by construction**: it has no D (no electroweak gauge, no photon) and no R
(no colour). Gravity only. Direct detection null — not because the cross section is small,
but because it is exactly zero.

**Parallel with ΛCDM:** the Standard Model does not predict DM. ΛCDM adds it as a
hypothetical particle (WIMP, axion, sterile neutrino) with free parameters. Here
m_DM ≈ 150 MeV emerges from the structure of the model.

## 9. CMB photons: not annihilation ash

In the standard model, CMB photons are the thermal remnant of matter-antimatter annihilation
in the primordial plasma. For every billion pairs that annihilate, one baryon survives.
The photon plasma is the "ash" of that annihilation.

In this model, this reading changes. DM does not annihilate (gauge = 0). Baryons annihilate
only in the quantity set by geometry (the fraction 1 − asym). The CMB photons are not
the residue of a thermal "bloodbath" — they are **the energy released by the primordial
black hole at the moment of the baryogenesis freeze-out**.

When the system crosses r_bario = 0.191 L and the asymmetry freezes, the horizon releases
a burst of thermal energy toward the brane — the photon bath that forms the CMB. That
energy inherits the perfect blackbody spectrum of Hawking radiation and the global causal
coordination of the 5D spacetime. The CMB does not need an annihilation thermalisation
process — it comes from the geometry.

**Why the CMB is so uniform (the horizon problem):**
In ΛCDM, the horizon problem (opposite regions of the sky have the same temperature despite
never having been in causal contact) is solved by inflation — a superluminal expansion in
the earliest moments. Here the problem vanishes: all regions of the brane are coupled to
the same black hole horizon. Thermal equilibrium is global by construction — inflation is
not needed.

hosting space: the squashed S³ with its two periodic phases.

The result is like Chladni figures: scatter sand randomly on a vibrating plate and it
arranges itself along precise lines determined by the shape of the plate. Scrambling is

Therefore: **the isotropic component of the flux (mass) undergoes full scrambling →
uniform thermal bath of the CMB. The phase structure (R) is a rigid isometry of the
spacetime — it cannot be scrambled because it is the background geometry itself.**

quiescence, not death.

at r_b > r_h with exact Friedmann equations. It is not a flaw — it is a misapplication
of static horizon reasoning to a dynamic case.

## 1. Basic architecture
The universe is a 3-brane on the horizon (Hopf S³) of a Myers–Perry–AdS5 black hole with two
angular momenta a1≠a2 [1]. The metric is the exact GLPP solution, with Ricci scalar R=−20/L²
verified [2]. The horizon radius coincides with the spin (r_h=a). The bulk flux is traceless null
dust; the dark energy has w=−1 from the AdS constant.

Fundamental geometric decomposition [3]: from the two spins one builds
- S = a1²+a2² (monopole, trace, mass) — symmetric combination;
- D = a1²−a2² (quadrupole, anisotropy) — antisymmetric combination.

## 2. Dark matter sector
DM is "pure-S": it receives the trace S (mass) but carries a trivial gauge representation, hence
zero gauge coupling [4]. The trace↔singlet identification is an interpretive hypothesis [S], not a
derivation: for g=0 a symmetry reason would be needed, still to be found.
Numerical result: Ω_dm/Ω_b = 1/asym = 5.62 against 5.41 observed (3.8%), mass-independent [5]. The
value asym=0.178 = tanh(Ω_H/2T_H) comes from the attractor spins [5].
Conditional experimental signature: null direct-detection if the identification holds [4].

## 3. Quadrupole sector
The boundary density is monopole S + quadrupole D with pure cos2χ dependence (no cos4χ), traceless
[6]. The brane observable is the proper ε = −Sᵗₜ. Israel (brane side) and holographic stress tensor

## 4. Higgs/scale sector
The VEV is a scale inherited from the bulk (initial condition), like the DM mass and the gauge

## 6. Geometry→fields theorem sector: what the geometry imposes
Operative distinction: what the geometry *imposes* versus what it *permits*. For the first time there
are two things in the "imposes" column, both qualitative properties (not numbers).

**Rank.** Theorem: if the Cartan of the gauge sector is realised by the geometric phases, then
gauge_rank = number of spins of the hole = 2 [30]. The hypothesis "Cartan=phases" has been made no
longer conditional but necessary, by exclusion [31]: the phases φ₁,φ₂ are the only two independent
periodic coordinates of the horizon (a1,a2 are parameters; r is not periodic; χ has rank 1 and
boundaries and is already committed to D). The literature confirms it: a squashed S³ has isometry
exactly U(1)×U(1), which is the Cartan of SO(4), and the Hopf phases are its two generators [32].
Falsifiable consequence: the rank of the Standard Model is 4 (2+1+1), a single two-spin hole hosts
rank 2 — colour SU(3), which has rank 2, fits exactly; the electroweak part (a further rank 2) must
come from the other channel (χ/D).

**Quantization.** The Cartan charges are the momenta conjugate to the phases, Q_i=−i∂/∂φ_i, with
integer eigenvalues because the phases are periodic [33]. Periodicity forces quantization: a fact not
explained in the Standard Model here has a geometric reason. It is real geometric quantization, not
yet "of colour" — it remains interpretive that the integers are the physical charges.

The Cartan is abelian with two generators; the common mode φ₁+φ₂ is the U(1) (trace), the relative
mode φ₁−φ₂ is the Cartan of the SU part (traceless, natural).
Remains input (untouched): which specific charges (the weight lattice) and the six off-diagonal
generators (H₂(S³)=0). The strong closure would be to derive the couplings exp(i(m₁φ₁+m₂φ₂)), not
yet done.

Matter forms at coupling, reading the geometry at the epoch a~1/m. The frame-dragging asymmetry

Energy balance [25]: S and D split all the rotational energy of the hole (they are the two spins);

Formation probability [26]: the measure on the phases is flat (torus, invariant under U(1)×U(1)),

Three attempts to anchor the asym inversion to known physics were discarded [28]: Higgs (minimum vs

## 8. Chirality sector: chiral fermions localised on the brane
Pure Kaluza–Klein (round sphere) does not produce chiral fermions: the angular momentum in the
compact part manifests in four dimensions as a charge of both handednesses, which double in second
quantization (Witten's theorem) [34]. This is the defect that marginalised Kaluza–Klein. Our
geometric "sense" (the J_i momentum linear in the two spins) is precisely the quantity that Witten
shows to be insufficient.

But our setup is not pure Kaluza–Klein: it is AdS5 with a brane. In that class chirality is a
standard mechanism (Randall–Sundrum, domain-wall fermions, warped fermions) [35]. The no-go does not
apply. There are two routes:

**Flux route (closed for us).** The Randjbar-Daemi–Shaposhnikov mechanism [36] generates chiral
families from a gauge flux on the internal manifold, with the number of families equal to the
monopole charge. It requires, however, a 2-cycle (H₂≠0, like S²). Our horizon is S³ with H₂=0: no
2-cycle, the Chern number is not defined, the mechanism does not start. It is the same topological
wall that excludes the off-diagonal generators of the colour sector.

**Domain-wall route (works).** The brane is a wall in the radial direction of the bulk; this route
does not use 2-cycles. A bulk fermion with a Yukawa coupling η·Ψ̄·F(φ)·Ψ to a wall-forming field has
a localised chiral zero-mode [37]. In our case the wall field is the flux that changes sign at
√(a1a2) (the frame-dragging inversion between the two spins). The full calculation gives a Schrödinger
potential of Pöschl–Teller type, exactly solvable: the left-handed zero-mode is normalizable and
localised, the right-handed one diverges — only one handedness survives, i.e. chirality. The excited
modes are all massive (the heavy tower) and do not affect the zero-mode.

The structural point: the slope of the kink is 2√(a1a2) and its amplitude scales as |D|=|a1²−a2²|. At
equal spins (a1=a2) the kink vanishes, the wall disappears, chirality is lost. Chirality requires
a1≠a2, the same condition that generates the quadrupole and the rank of the gauge sector: a single
origin — the asymmetry of the two spins — for three distinct sectors. And the kink is not put in by
hand (as in the other models) but is produced by the rotating geometry.

Honest limits: the Yukawa coupling is assumed in the standard form, not derived; the calculation is
the zero-mode profile, not the full Dirac index, so the number and precise handedness of the families
remain open — and that calculation, via the index, remains blocked by the same H₂(S³)=0.
The Ṁ(t) profile of the bulk flux is not derivable from the model and remains open. On it depend
w(z), equipartition, and the temporal profile. Related: the microstate→radiation map is a universal
open problem (firewall, islands, Page curve), not specific to the model.

## 9. Temperature sector and thermal destiny
Two temperatures connected by the Tolman gradient (cont.203, cont.253):

**T_mat ~ 1/a ~ 1/r_h** (the plasma, what we observe): falls monotonically as the universe expands.
No minimum, no rise. Consistent with observation (the universe cools). Corrected error: the rise
of T_geom was mistaken for the temperature of matter — they are the two different ends of the gradient.

**T_geom = (1+2r_h²)/(4πr_h)** (horizon temperature): has a minimum at the hinge r_h = L/√2 =
0.707L, then rises again. The rise occurs because at large r_h the hole "feels the walls" of AdS
(the curvature compresses it rather than letting it expand freely). The rise of T_geom does not
heat the plasma: they are different ends of the gradient.

**Re-encounter** at r_h = √((4π−1)/2) = 2.405L: the two ends equalise and the Tolman gradient
inverts. Beyond that point the horizon becomes hotter than the matter and can re-radiate toward
the brane. Re-fusion becomes possible — not because T_mat rises, but because the gradient inverts.

**Thresholds (in units of L):** frame-dragging inversion 0.173L; hinge 0.707L; re-encounter 2.405L.

**Thermal destiny:** controlled by the tail of the flux Ṁ(t→∞).

**L** fixed by dark energy (= AdS constant of the bulk, input not prediction). L ≈ 9.5×10²⁵ m.
